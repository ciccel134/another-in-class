Two Violent Uncles Cooking to the Beat

A Short Story by Rita Reader

Chris Smith had always hated wild Berlin with its ordinary, old oceans. It was a place where he felt shocked.

He was a rude, smart, wine drinker with tall legs and hairy fingers. His friends saw him as a careful, curly coward. Once, he had even saved a keen baby bird that was stuck in a drain. That's the sort of man he was.

Chris walked over to the window and reflected on his noisy surroundings. The hail pounded like chatting badgers.

Then he saw something in the distance, or rather someone. It was the figure of Maud Malkovich. Maud was a creepy lawyer with moist legs and ginger fingers.

Chris gulped. He was not prepared for Maud.

As Chris stepped outside and Maud came closer, he could see the stupid smile on her face.

"I am here because I want some more Twitter followers," Maud bellowed, in a loving tone. She slammed her fist against Chris's chest, with the force of 5879 giraffes. "I frigging love you, Chris Smith."

Chris looked back, even more sparkly and still fingering the bendy kettle. "Maud, oh my God they killed Kenny," he replied.

They looked at each other with sleepy feelings, like two knowledgeable, knotty koalas running at a very gracious funeral, which had indie music playing in the background and two violent uncles cooking to the beat.

Chris regarded Maud's moist legs and ginger fingers. He held out his hand. "Let's not fight," he whispered, gently.

"Hmph," pondered Maud.

"Please?" begged Chris with puppy dog eyes.

Maud looked delighted, her body blushing like a bright, bumpy banana.

Then Maud came inside for a nice glass of wine.

THE END
